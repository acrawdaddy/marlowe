Good questions

Before you join.

What is care coordination?

Care coordination is having one trained person organize all of your healthcare in one place. They schedule appointments, gather records from every doctor, track medications, and explain each step in plain language — so nothing falls through the cracks between providers.

Are you replacing my doctors?

No. We don't practice medicine. We make sure your doctors have what they need and that nothing falls between them. Your physicians stay exactly who they are.

Is this covered by Medicare or insurance?

Marlowe is a private membership, paid directly by you — which is what lets us answer only to you. We coordinate with your existing coverage and help you use it well.

What if I'm healthy and just want to stay organized?

That's one of the most common reasons people join. Many members are active and well — they just want one place for their care and one person to handle the busywork.

My parent lives far away. Can you help?

Yes. Much of our work is for adult children coordinating care for a parent in another state. We become the steady local-feeling contact, and keep you informed.

Do I have to learn an app?

No. Reach us by phone, email, or text — however you prefer. The secure record-keeping happens quietly in the background.

Who is my advisor?

An experienced care coordinator, often with a nursing background, assigned to your household — so you always speak to someone who knows your story. They're backed by a clinical team and a 24/7 line.
